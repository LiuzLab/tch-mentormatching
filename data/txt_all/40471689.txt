overview

While pursuing my PhD, I had the honor of learning from world-class anatomy
educators at Indiana University School of Medicine. This experience changed
the trajectory of my career, and I began teaching within anatomical science,
bone biology, and cell biology courses for the final 3 years of my PhD. As a
more recent student, I integrate concepts and techniques that were successful
in my own scholastic career into my educational strategies to provide a unique
perspective on teaching. My research areas of interest include incorporation
of active learning approaches into the curriculum of previously unexplored
populations (such as graduate students) and assessment of these approaches in
comparison to traditional didactic curriculum.
