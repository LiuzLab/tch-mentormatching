overview

Investigator, Howard Hughes Medical Institute, Baylor College of Medicine  
Professor, Departments of Molecular and Human Genetics and Neuroscience  
Director, Program in Developmental Biology  
  
I am interested in (1) providing a better fundamental understanding of the
biology that governs the proper function and maintenance of neurons in aging
adults (2) developing tools that can be applied to most genes to control
transcript and protein levels in adult neurons to assess which proteins are
required for neuronal survival and proper function (3) creating genome wide
libraries to manipulate most genes in vivo. My lab uses the fruit fly
Drosophila melanogaster as a model system because most biological processes
are evolutionarily conserved and studies in fruit flies provide many important
clues about the aging process in animals and human diseases.
