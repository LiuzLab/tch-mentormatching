overview

Lefkothea P. Karaviti, M.D., Ph.D., is Professor of Pediatrics, Endocrinology
and Diabetes. She has more than 23 years of experience treating pediatric
patients at Texas Children’s Hospital (TCH)/BCM. She received her M.D. from
National University in Athens, Greece, and her Ph.D. from UCLA, where she
trained in sexual differentiation of the brain and collaborated with the
pediatric endocrinology division. She did her residency and post-graduate
training at Cornell Medical College (Cornell University), where her focus was
on pediatric endocrinology and diabetes, as well as congenital adrenal
hyperplasia (CAH) under renowned expert Maria New. She currently trains
fellows in the development of evidence-based and best-practice guidelines for
multidisciplinary approaches to disorders of sexual development (DSD) from the
neonatal period to adolescence. She is a member of the Pediatric Endocrinology
Society, American Pediatric Society, American Diabetes Association, American
Association of Clinical Endocrinologists, and the Endocrine Society. Her
clinical practice involves patients with type I diabetes, growth disorders,
CAH, and DSD. Dr. Karaviti was a pioneer in the establishment of a
multidisciplinary approach to treating DSD, adding an ethicist to the team and
incorporating education of parents so they could participate in an informed
decision-making process, as well as in the development of guidelines for
treating DSD.
