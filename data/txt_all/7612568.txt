research activities and funding

(Dr. Joseph Spinner)Jul 1, 2022 - Jun 30, 2024Enduring Hearts  
Non-HLA Antibodies in Pediatric Heart Transplantation  
Role Description: Investigation of the presence and role of non-HLA antibodies
in pediatric heart transplantation rejection and coronary allograft
vasculopathy  
Role: Principal Investigator

AHA 824138 (Dr. James Martin)Jul 1, 2021 - Jun 30, 2024American Heart
Association and Enduring Hearts  
Early Detection of Cardiac Allograft Vasculopathy in Post-Transplant Hearts
via Single-Cell Genomics  
Role Description: Goal is to identify coronary allograft vasculopathy at early
stages using single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) to study heart tissue
obtained from children with heart disease which may allow for early detection
and subsequent treatment.  
Role: Co-Investigator  
  
* * *  
  
(Dr. David Rosenthal)Jan 1, 2021 - Dec 31, 2023Enduring Hearts and Additional
Ventures  
Can HLA data be used to support Precision Medicine in Pediatric Heart
Transplantation? An exploratory analysis  
Role Description: Goal is to analyze the impact of HLA donor/recipient
matching and pre-transplant HLA antibodies on survival and adverse events.
Goal is also to develop a decision support tool for predicting the results of
a virtual crossmatch at the time of a donor offer.  
  
* * *  
  
(Dr. Joseph Spinner)Jul 19, 2019 - Dec 31, 2020Texas Children’s Hospital Heart
Center Innovation Award  
Novel Noninvasive Detection of Cardiac Allograft Rejection after Pediatric
Heart Transplantation  
Role Description: Goal is detect the presence of allogeneic chimerism in the
serum and plasma of patients after heart transplant to detect or predict
cardiac allograft rejection.  
Role: Principal Investigator  
  
* * *  
  
W81XWH-18-1-0557 (Dr. Joseph Spinner)Sep 1, 2018 - Feb 28, 2022Department of
Defense  
Composition, Function, and Role of the Intestinal Microbiome in Pediatric
Heart Failure and Heart Transplantation  
Role Description: The primary objective is to characterize and determine the
role of the intestinal microbiome in children who have undergone heart
transplantation. We hypothesize that dysbiosis increases the risk of post-
transplant complications  
Role: Principal Investigator
