(CDMRP) Peer Reviewed Medical Research Program (PRMRP) Investigator-Initiated
Research Award  
Risk of hepatocellular cancer after virological cure with direct acting
antiviral agents in individuals with hepatitis C  
Role Description: The goal of this study is to examine the risk and
determinants of hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) in patients with chronic
hepatitis C (CHC) virologically cured with new direct acting antiviral agent
(DAA) regimens and to identify cost-effective strategies for early detection
of HCC in those patients.  
Role: Co-Investigator

1P01CA263025-01 (Kanwal)Apr 1, 2022 - Mar 1, 2027NIH  
Prevention of Hepatocellular Carcinoma Related to Metabolic Syndrome, Project
2  
Role Description: The overarching goal of this project is to reduce the burden
of hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC)- related mortality through better
understanding of contemporary risk factors (e.g., phenotypic, biochemical and
genetic markers of metabolic dysfunction) and protective factors (e.g.,
chemoprevention, HCC surveillance) of HCC related to metabolic (dysfunction)
fatty liver disease (MAFLD), and identifying the comparative effectiveness of
prevention strategies in individuals with MAFLD. The goal of Project 2 is to
reduce hepatocellular cancer (HCC)-related mortality by investigating
personalized chemoprevention in individuals with metabolic-associated fatty
liver disease (MAFLD).  
Role: Co-Investigator
