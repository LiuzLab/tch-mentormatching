overview

Dr. Brewer is Professor of Pediatrics and former Head of the Pediatric Renal
Section at Baylor College of Medicine and is currently Medical Director of
Kidney Transplantation at Texas Children’s Hospital. She is recipient of the
Baylor College of Medicine Master Clinician Award, the highest institution-
wide honor for faculty contributions to patient care. She is an
internationally known expert in pediatric renal diseases, hypertension,
dialysis, and transplantation. She received the American Academy of Pediatrics
Henry L. Barnett Award for Lifetime Achievement in Pediatric Nephrology in
2022. She was the 2010 recipient of the Founder's Award of the American
Society of Pediatric Nephrology (ASPN) for unique and lasting contributions to
pediatric nephrology and is past ASPN president, past Council member and long-
time member and past chair of the Public Policy Committee. She has been a
Council Member of the International Pediatric Nephrology Association (IPNA)
and organizer of two IPNA International Workshops on Pediatric Hypertension in
2004 and 2007. She served two terms as a Board member of the Renal Physicians
Association and continues as member and past chair of the Healthcare Payment
Committee. She has been a member of the Lowe Syndrome Association Medical and
Scientific Advisory Board since 1999. She is a past member of the American
Board of Pediatrics serving on the General Examinations and Maintenance of
Certification Committees for over twenty years. She is currently a member and
chair of the American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) Coding and Nomenclature
Committee and alternate representative of the AAP to the AMA-Relative Value
Scale Update Committee (RUC). She is past member and chair of the United
Network of Organ Sharing (UNOS) Pediatric Transplant Committee, past member of
the UNOS Kidney Transplant Committee and Policy Oversight Committee and past
member of the UNOS/OPTN Board of Directors. She enjoys being a mentor to
fellows in pediatric nephrology fellowship training at Baylor and to pre-
medical students at Rice University.
