overview

I am a molecular epidemiologist whose research interests include structural
birth defects and childhood cancer. I use large-scale, population-based data
in combination with metabolomics and genomics approaches to identify factors
associated with these phenotypes, and to understand the molecular basis for
their tendency to co-occur. My pre-doctoral work was in nutritional
epidemiology, and I maintain an interest in the associations of nutritional
status with birth defects and childhood cancer. I am active in several
national and international organizations, including the Childrenâ€™s Oncology
Group, Society for Birth Defects Research and Prevention, and Childhood
Leukemia International Consortium.
